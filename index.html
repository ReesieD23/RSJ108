How to conduct a successful journalistic interview:

Assembly time: 1-3 hours
Awesome rating: 100% awesome

Do you want to conduct your first journalistic interview for a magazine article or news story you’re writing? Well, look no further. After applying these tips, you will feel prepared going into it!

  • Things You'll Need

  • A writing utensil (pencil or pen is recommended)
  • Paper (Lined preferred)
  • An Electronic Device (such as a computer or cell phone)
  • Internet Access
  • Friendly attitude

  1. Do your research on the person.

    Before you can write out good questions to ask someone who you are interviewing for an article, podcast, or another medium, you need to gather all the information you can.

      2. Write or type out your questions to prepare for the interview.

  • Write down your objective for the interview.

    Once you know who this person you’re interviewing is, it’s a good idea to write down what you want from the interview.

  • Ask open-ended questions.

    You’re interviewing a person to gain knowledge on a subject. If you want to create a dialogue, this means that questions that don’t let the interviewee simply answer “yes” or “no” will help you.

  • Ask a reflection question.

    Questions that make your interviewee look back and share a story or example are great to keep things going and give you useful information.

  • Ask questions that elicit an emotional response.

    Like open-ended questions, you’ll want to develop a few that will elicit an emotional response.
